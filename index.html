Rifter

The Routine Shifter

When you have consistent routines at inconsistent times

About

Start your routine at any time of day or night.

Create and save your schedule! It will stay saved on your device, so you only need to enter it once. No logging in, account, or app needed!

Choose a start or an end time and see what time to do each task.

Everything is saved and calculated on your device: your information doesn't get sent anywhere and isn't viewable by anyone else (unless you hand someone your phone).

Tips

  • Tap "Calculate & Save" to save your schedule and calculate start and end times for each task. If you don't enter an overall start/end time, Rifter will just save your schedule - make sure to save before refreshing or closing Rifter!
  • You can pick an overall start or end time, but not both. If you enter both, Rifter will calculate your schedule using the start time.
  • To calculate from a new start/end time, enter the new time then tap "Calculate & Save" again.
  • "Clear Times" will remove the overall start/end time and the start/end times for each task.
  • "Clear Everything" will delete your schedule and reset everything. This can't be undone, so be careful!
  • On phones and tablets, you can pin Rifter to your home screen by opening the browser menu (usually "⋮" in the top right corner) and tapping "Add to Home Screen."

Problems?

  • If anything weird happens, refresh the page and try again.
  • Rifter won't work on Safari (sorry).
  • Rifter's been tested on Firefox and Chrome, but may or may not work on other browsers.
  • Only one schedule available for now.
  • Hours and minutes for a task must each be a whole number 0 - 360. Any other numbers will be rounded to the nearest valid number.
  • Corrupted schedules are automatically deleted - typically this is due to invalid input.
  • Emojis in task names sometimes cause issues (not sure why yet). Try and avoid them for now.
  • If you're running into consistent problems, send me a message or create an issue on Github (make sure to check there isn't one already first!).
